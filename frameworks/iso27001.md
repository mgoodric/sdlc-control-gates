# ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (Annex A controls)

This file shows which Annex A controls the gate catalog produces evidence for, and how the evidence satisfies the control.

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 has 93 Annex A controls organized into four themes: Organizational (37), People (8), Physical (14), Technological (34). The catalog produces evidence primarily for the Technological theme, with significant coverage of Organizational change-management and project-management controls.

## How an auditor uses this

ISO 27001 auditors look for a documented Information Security Management System (ISMS) and evidence that named controls are operating. For the SDLC-shaped controls:

- **A.8.32 (change management):** the auditor wants to see a documented procedure and evidence it operates. The gate catalog IS the documented procedure; the gate evidence is the operation.
- **A.8.29 (security testing):** evidence of testing throughout development and at acceptance. `tests-passed` and `policy-passed` cover the development side. `ai-adversarial-test` extends this to AI behavior.
- **A.8.25 (SDL):** evidence that security activities are integrated through the lifecycle, not bolted on. The PDLC gates (`build-decision-recorded`, `risk-review-attached`) are this integration.
- **A.5.3 (segregation of duties):** evidence that author, reviewer, deployer, and approver are not the same identity for high-risk changes. The catalog enforces this through `change-reviewed` (non-author reviewer) and `deploy-authorized` (separate authorization step).

## What this catalog does NOT cover

The 93 Annex A controls go well beyond what an SDLC produces:

- **Organizational theme (most of it):** policies, supplier management, classification, asset management, access control policy, awareness, business continuity
- **People theme:** screening, terms of employment, awareness, disciplinary process
- **Physical theme:** all of it
- **Other Technological controls:** A.8.1–A.8.14 (asset management, access control, MFA, encryption), A.8.17–A.8.24 (privileged access, network controls, encryption keys, web filtering), A.8.34 (audit testing protection)

You need separate evidence sources and an actual ISMS for those. The catalog is a tool inside a broader ISMS, not a replacement for one.

## Notes for AI-DLC mappings

The AI-DLC gates map to A.8.29 (security testing) and A.8.33 (test information) as best-fit. A.5.7 (threat intelligence) maps to `ai-adversarial-test` because the adversarial corpus IS applied threat intelligence about AI attack patterns. None of these is a clean fit — they were written before generative AI evaluations existed.

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AI Management Systems) is the standards-side answer to this gap. As 42001 audits become common, the AI-DLC gates will get their own framework file. See `docs/ai-dlc-extension.md`.
